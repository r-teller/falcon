# P2-02 — Field rations (cadence, lifetime, score, despawn)

## Summary
Field rations are a Phase-2-onward bonus pickup themed as a higher-tier supply drop. Spawn cadence: uniform random 20–35 s starting at Phase 2 entry. Lifetime on field: 8 s, then despawn (vanish) uncollected. Pickup value: +5 score, +1 segment. At most one field ration on board at a time (per §3.25). Render in coyote brown (`#8b6f47`) with a small gold accent (`#b8860b`) to read as a higher-tier care package, distinguishable from the supply cache.

Per PRD §3.25 (max 1 on board); §6.3 (spec); §10.1 cadence matrix; §10.2 spawn rules; §13.6 fairness invariants.

## Persona
Primary: End User (bonus pickup chase-or-skip decision).

## Acceptance Criteria
### End User
- [ ] Field rations begin spawning within ~35 s of Phase 2 entry.
- [ ] Picking up a field ration adds 5 to the score and grows the patrol by 1 segment.
- [ ] An uncollected field ration vanishes after 8 s on the field.
- [ ] At most one field ration is on the board at any time.
- [ ] Field rations look distinct from supply caches (different color, optional gold accent — clearly a higher-tier item).

### System
- [ ] Cadence interval is uniform random in [20000, 35000] ms.
- [ ] Spawn rejection skips cells occupied by patrol segments and the active supply cache.
- [ ] When `state.phase === 1`, no field rations spawn (cadence timer doesn't start until Phase 2 entry).
- [ ] After Phase 2 entry, the cadence timer pauses during `state.paused` (FIN-04 wires the freeze; for this bead, OK to leave timer ticking; FIN-04 will fix).

## Patterns to Reuse
- Time-driven scheduler pattern: `state.fieldRationNextSpawn = now + rand(min, max)` set after spawn or despawn.
- Same rejection-sampling helper from FN-05 (extend it to exclude `state.fieldRation` too).

## Testing Strategy
- Manual: reach Phase 2, wait up to 35 s, verify a field ration appears. Eat it, verify score +5, length +1, new cadence starts.
- Manual: spawn a field ration (or wait), don't pick it up, verify it vanishes after ~8 s.
- Regression: supply caches keep working in Phase 2.
